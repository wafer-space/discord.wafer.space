[2025-10-02 8:28 a.m.] valgamaa
It seems to be quite normal these days that the steppers stay within the radius of the wafer with a small margin. I guess there is no point in exposing to the edge where part of the chip lies off-chip, that is a waste of time. This isn't (shouldn't be) because the processing 'quality' deteriorates towards the edge, just a practical limit for handling the wafer. Having said that, I did one see a bipolar process that used epitaxy for the base and growth rate was higher towards the centre of the wafer. This resulted in devices where each wafer had the full process spread depending on where on the wafer the die were taken from; higher ft at the edges, higher Vceo towards the centre. It was soon discontinued!
